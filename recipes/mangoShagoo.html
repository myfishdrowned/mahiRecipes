Mango Sago is a delightful Asian dessert known for its creamy and tropical flavor profile. Originating from Hong Kong, this dessert combines sweet mango puree, chewy tapioca pearls (sago), and a rich base of coconut milk or cream. Served chilled, itâ€™s a refreshing treat that is especially popular during the summer months. This dessert balances the sweetness of ripe mangoes with the creamy texture of the coconut base, and the tiny tapioca pearls provide a fun and chewy contrast. Its vibrant color and luscious taste make Mango Sago a favorite at gatherings and celebrations.

Mango Sago is more than just a dessert; it's a sensory delight. The bright orange hues of fresh mangoes create a visual appeal, while the light sweetness of the coconut milk adds a subtle depth to the flavor. The soft tapioca pearls, often likened to tiny jelly balls, create an interesting texture that makes each spoonful a unique experience. This dessert is not only delicious but also incredibly simple to make, with a few easily accessible ingredients.

Traditionally served in small bowls or glasses, Mango Sago is perfect for a variety of occasions. Whether it's a weekend family dinner or a festive celebration, this dessert never fails to impress. You can even customize it by adding pomelo, lychee, or other tropical fruits for an extra twist.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 2 large ripe mangoes (peeled, deseeded, and diced)
  • 1/2 cup small tapioca pearls (sago)

For serving

  • Ice cubes
  • mint leaves

Steps

  1. Boil 4 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Add the tapioca pearls to the boiling water and cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Once the pearls turn translucent, drain and rinse them under cold water to remove excess starch.
  4. Set aside in a bowl of cold water to prevent sticking.
